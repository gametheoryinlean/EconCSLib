# Sequential Equilibrium

A pair $(\beta,\mu)$ is a sequential equilibrium if it is weak Bayesian perfect and
there exists a sequence of completely mixed behavioral profiles $\beta^n$ converging
to $\beta$ such that the Bayes-rule beliefs $\nu_{\beta^n}$ converge to $\mu$.

Thus off-path beliefs must arise as limits of beliefs generated by fully mixed
trembling strategies.

## References

- [MFoGT, Def. 6.4.5] Laraki, Renault, and Sorin, *Mathematical Foundations of Game Theory*. Sequential equilibrium as weak Bayesian perfection plus consistency from completely mixed trembles.
